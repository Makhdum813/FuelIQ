How Calculations Work

Every figure starts from your Original Mileage (before E20) and a Mileage Drop % (how much worse it gets after E20). Everything else — cost per km, monthly impact, distance lost — is derived from just those two numbers plus petrol price and how much you drive. See the About tab for where the drop-percentage ranges come from, or the Formulas panel inside Calculator for the exact math.

How to Use Fuel Logs

Tell it what you actually did at the pump: how much you paid and the price per litre — litres are calculated for you automatically (matching how most people actually buy fuel in India). Log a few fill-ups against a saved vehicle and the app derives your real measured mileage, which you can pull straight into the Calculator with one click.

How Reports Are Generated

Reports tab is where every export lives. Print/PDF builds a dedicated, print-only report layout (not a screenshot of the dashboard) with charts, tables, and insights. CSV exports the same underlying data as a spreadsheet-friendly file. Both always reflect whatever is currently set in the Calculator tab.

Yes, once installed as an app (or visited once with an internet connection) — everything except the vehicle-image lookup and WhatsApp share works fully offline.

Understanding E20

📘What is E20, and why does it change your mileage?

E20 is petrol blended with 20% ethanol, up from the 10% (E10) blend most pumps sold until recently. India completed the shift to E20 availability nationwide as part of the government's ethanol blending programme, which moved faster than its original 2030 target.

The reason mileage dips isn't a fault in your engine — it's basic chemistry. Ethanol carries noticeably less energy per litre than petrol: roughly 21 MJ/L for ethanol versus about 32 MJ/L for petrol, per data from the IEA's Advanced Motor Fuels programme and the US Department of Energy. Blend in 20% of a lower-energy fuel, and the engine has to burn a bit more of it to produce the same power — so your km/L figure drops, even though nothing mechanical has changed.

The good news: ARAI's 2026 fleet testing found that drivability, startability, and acceleration were unaffected in compatible vehicles — fuel economy is the only measurable cost.

Assumptions & Limitations
  • The mileage drop is modelled as a simple proportional reduction in km/L, matching how ARAI and industry sources describe the effect.
  • E20 and the previous blend are assumed to cost the same per litre at the pump, which reflects current Indian pricing. If a price differential is introduced later, actual extra cost would differ.
  • Your "Original Mileage" should ideally be your own real-world figure rather than a manufacturer's lab-tested number, since the drop percentage is applied on top of it.
  • Driving distance, style, and conditions are held constant between the before/after comparison — only the fuel changes. In practice, tyre pressure, traffic, and maintenance affect mileage far more than the blend itself.
  • This tool covers petrol vehicles moving from E10 to E20. It does not model E85, flex-fuel, or diesel vehicles.
Frequently Asked Questions
Does E20 petrol actually reduce mileage?

Yes. Ethanol holds less energy per litre than petrol, so a 20% ethanol blend delivers slightly less energy per litre overall. ARAI's 2026 testing measured a 2–6% drop in fuel consumption across a range of vehicle ages, and drivability was unaffected in compatible vehicles.

Is E20 petrol mandatory in India?

India rolled out E20 availability nationwide in a phased manner from April 2023, reaching the 20% blending target during the 2025–26 ethanol supply year — ahead of the original 2030 schedule set out in the National Biofuel Policy.

What efficiency-drop percentage should I use for my car?

Match your vehicle to the closest row in the reference table above. Newer, E20-calibrated vehicles sit around 1–2%, while older E0-designed vehicles running on E20 can see 6–7% for four-wheelers or 3–4% for two-wheelers.

Is E20 bad for my car's engine?

ARAI's long-term trials — covering 40,000 km for passenger vehicles and 20,000 km for two-wheelers — found no engine breakdowns attributable to E20 in compatible vehicles. Older vehicles not designed for higher ethanol content may face compatibility issues with certain hoses, seals, or fuel-system components, which is a separate concern from the mileage impact this calculator estimates.

Does E20 affect two-wheelers more than cars?

Generally the opposite — NITI Aayog's roadmap estimated a larger drop for older four-wheelers (6–7%) than for older two-wheelers (3–4%), since two-wheeler engines were typically re-tuned earlier in the transition.

Will the mileage loss improve as newer vehicles arrive?

Yes — vehicles specifically engineered and calibrated for E20 from the factory are expected to see a much smaller drop (around 1–2%) than older vehicles retrofitted or simply tolerant of the blend.
